    P

    Person who lacks mental capacity in accordance with the Mental Capacity in accordance with the Metal Capacity Act (Cap 177A, 2010 Rev Ed)

    Metal Capacity

    In accordance to sections 4 & 5 of the Metal Capacity Act, a person who lacks mental capacity is unable to make a decision for himself/ herself due to an impairment of, or a disturbance in the functin of, the mind or brain

    He/she is unable to make a decision for himself/herself if he/she is unable to understand, retain or wigh the information relevant to the decision, or communicate his/her decision

STAGE 2 | 7 Question

Tell us more about P

1. Has P been medically certified as lacking mental capacity to make decisions?

As part of the deputyship application, P must be medically certified to lack mental capacity to make decisions from himself/herself. Please consult a medical practitioner and request for a medical report under the Mental Capacity Act thereafter.

2. Does P have a Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) ?

You can access the Office of the Public Guardian's e-services portal in the Resources tab to perform a search request for Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA).

if "Yes": You need not apply for Deputyship as P has appointed a Donee under the Lasting Power of Affairs to make decisions on his/her behalf.

3. Is P is residing in a nursing / old folks/ werlfare home? ?

Please note that you are required to serve a Notice to Relevant Persons, together with your deputyship application, to the nursing/ old folks/ welfare home where P is residing at.
