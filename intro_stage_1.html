  • GLOSSARY

    Successor deputy

    A person who takes over your duties as a deputy if you are unable to properly perform your duties. This is someone who steps in to become your child, ward or family member’s (“P”) deputy in the event that you (or any of your joint deputy (or deputies,) if applicable) are incapable of continuing to function as a deputy. This may be because of loss of mental capacity, the death of the deputy, and bankruptcy of the deputy and other reasons.

STAGE 1 | 8 Questions

Tell us what happened

1. Were you informed by the hospital or relevant government agency (i.e. HDB / CPF) to apply for deputyship?

2. Have you explored any of the followings for your deputyship application:

  • Legal Aid Bureou (LAB)

  • Integrated Family Application Management System (iFAM

  • Assisted Deputyship Application Programme (SPED) School (ADAP)

  • Private Lawyers

You are highly encouraged to explore these options before embarking on your deputyship application.

3. Who is seeking to be appointed as a Deputy?

4. Will there be a successor deputy for the application?

5. Are you seeking appointment of 1 deputy and 1 successor deputy?

The interactive roadmap is targeted at appointment of 1 deputy and 1 successor deputy. If you are seeking for appointment of more than 1 deputy and 1 successor deputy, please seek legal advice

6. Is the prospective deputy and successor deputy at least 21 years old?

The prospective deputy and/or successor deputy has to be at least 21 years of age

7. Do you forsee anyone contesting the application?

This interactive roadmap is targeted at straightforward and uncontested deputyship applications. If you foresee any persons objecting to your application, you are highly encouraged to seek legal advice from a lawyer.

8. Is there anyone who might have an interest in this deputyship application, including P's immediate family members?

Please note that you are required to inform them of your application and obtain their written consent, which must be sworm or affirmed before Commision for Oath.
